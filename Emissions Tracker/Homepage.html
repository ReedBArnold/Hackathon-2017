The past


The Earth has sustained a steady stream of Carbdon Dioxide (CO2) being poured into the atmosphere for millions of years from several sources. Data has been gathered from carbon dated icebergs that contained records showing the CO2 emission levels that were present in the atomosphere. The standard format used is parts per million (ppm) to establish a base level that has been present for the past 2 million years.


In the course of the last 650k years, Earth atmospheric CO2 levels has never been above 300 ppm, and we know that through mineral deposits, fossils, and arctic ice leaving telltale predictable signs of how much CO2 must have been in the air at the time. Today, CO2 is over 400 ppm.


Seen from the huge level spike over the past few hundred years the conclusion must be made that humans are the cause of this catastrophic rise. We as a species burn fossil fuels, destroy forests, constantly pollute the oceans, and rip all the natural resources we have left on this planet.

THE Present


The earth has been steadily increasing at an alarming rate for the past 50+ years with no current end in site! These current levels are an historic all time high which is leading scientists to some dangerous conclusions about the future of our planet if we don't start making some serious changes soon.


GOAL: We must strive together as human beings to reduce our own daily CO2 emissions and raise awarness of this alarming global trend. The amount of CO2 that we release on a daily basis may not seem like much but put together with all humans on earth and it accounts for a large percentage of the global daily output. If we were able to change the culture across the planet about the wasteful emissions we give off without even realizing then we still have a chance to fight this seemingly impossible battle. The world needs us all to step up as one to reduce our daily CO2 emissions!
